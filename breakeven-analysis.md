# McPherson Lab - Breakeven Productivity Analysis

## Cost Per Trainee

### Direct Compensation
- **Base Gross Salary**: $84,706 (to fund $70,800 base package after taxes)
- **Employer Portion of FICA**: $6,480 (7.65% of $84,706)
- **Benefits Administration**: ~$1,000 (estimated)
- **Total Direct Cost**: $92,186

### Indirect Costs (Estimated)
- **Office Space/Lab Access**: $3,000/year
- **Equipment/Supplies**: $2,000/year
- **Software/Tools**: $1,500/year
- **Training & Development**: $1,500/year
- **Insurance**: $1,000/year
- **Administrative Overhead**: $2,000/year
- **Total Indirect Cost**: $11,000

### Total Annual Base Cost Per Trainee
**$103,186**

## Revenue Sharing Model

In addition to the base cost, trainees receive **10% of all revenue they generate**. This means:
- For every $100,000 in revenue generated, the lab receives $90,000 and pays the trainee $10,000
- The lab's breakeven point must account for both base costs and revenue sharing

### True Breakeven Calculation

For the lab to break even:
- Lab's 90% share must cover base costs + indirect costs

**Breakeven Formula**: 
- Lab Revenue Share = Base Costs
- 90% × Total Revenue = $103,186
- **Total Revenue Required = $114,651**

At breakeven, trainee receives:
- Base package: $70,800 (value of benefits/stipends)
- Revenue share: $11,465 (10% of $114,651)
- **Total trainee compensation at breakeven**: $82,265

## Risk Factors & Mitigation

### Risks
1. **Market competition**: Established competitors in CE and lab services
2. **Regulatory changes**: Changes in CE requirements or lab regulations
3. **Client concentration**: Over-reliance on few large clients
4. **Quality control**: Maintaining high standards with growth

### Mitigation Strategies
1. Differentiate through specialized expertise and personalized service
2. Stay informed on regulatory landscape and adapt quickly
3. Diversify client base across multiple sectors
4. Implement robust quality assurance processes
5. Regular trainee performance reviews and professional development

## Conclusion

Each trainee requires **$114,651** in annual revenue (1,195 subscribers @ $8/mo) to break even. This can be achieved through:
- **Primary Focus**: Growing newsletter subscriber base to 1,195+ per newsletter at $8/month
- **Secondary Revenue**: 5-10 service contracts (antibiogram or outbreak detection)
- **Supplementary Income**: Tiered pricing, sponsorships, or live workshops

The 7 PI newsletters (diseaseprevention, toxicology, thehealthofnations, scienceeducation, sciencecommunication, primaryeducation, epidemic) plus the Medium Friend Partner Program contribute an additional $24,720/year ($22,248 lab share) across 15 total newsletters (8 trainee + 7 PI), diversifying and supplementing lab revenue while trainees build their subscriber bases.

With newsletter growth to 1,195+ subscribers each, trainees can exceed the breakeven point and contribute to long-term lab sustainability and growth.
